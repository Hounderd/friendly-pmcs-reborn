Friendly PMCs Reborn turns SPT 4.x into a persistent squad experience: build a roster, bring selected PMCs into raids, command them in combat, recruit new allies, and manage follower gear through Tarkov's own social and inventory screens.

This is the active SPT 4.x rebuild of FriendlyPMC. The goal is not a quick legacy port. It is a cleaner squad loop built around persistent roster data, raid-to-raid equipment saveback, Messenger-based management, social profile integration, and practical follower inventory control.

[tab='Overview']

A real squad layer for SPT 4.x

Friendly PMCs Reborn lets you treat selected PMCs as long-term squad members instead of disposable one-raid helpers. Followers can be stored in a roster, configured for autojoin, spawned back into raids, and managed through in-game UI surfaces that already feel native to Tarkov.

Core loop

  • Create or recruit squad members.
  • Choose who joins raids automatically.
  • Use hotkeys to direct followers during raids.
  • Save follower progression and equipment back into the persistent roster.
  • Open follower profiles and inventories from Tarkov's social UI.

Current focus

  • SPT 4.x support first.
  • Generic PMC followers first.
  • Persistent gear and progression.
  • Practical squad management inside the game.
  • Compatibility hardening with modern AI stacks where possible.

[tab='Current Features']

Persistent squad management

  • Server-side roster records for managed followers.
  • Follower nicknames, side, autojoin state, kit mode, level, experience, and profile snapshot data.
  • Autojoin-enabled followers spawn back into raids as your active squad.
  • Follower equipment and progression save back into the roster pipeline between raids.
  • Health is reset between raids while gear/progression persistence remains the core long-term loop.

Messenger and social UI

  • Followers appear through Tarkov's social surfaces.
  • Follower profiles can be opened from the friends/profile flow.
  • Follower profile screens expose OPEN INVENTORY for direct gear management.
  • Profile refresh work keeps visible follower paper-doll gear closer to the live persisted inventory state after successful inventory changes.

Follower inventory management

  • Dedicated follower inventory management screen.
  • Visible equipped slots and carry containers.
  • Move items from player inventory into follower storage.
  • Move follower items back to the player stash/inventory flow.
  • Equip compatible items into follower slots such as headwear, face cover, headset, armor, rig, backpack, pockets, secure container, holster, scabbard, primary weapon, and secondary weapon.
  • Drag/drop support is in progress and already has a first functional pass with drag preview behavior.
  • Inventory move validation reports clearer errors for incompatible slots and full containers.

Recruitment and raid control

  • Compatible same-side allies can be recruited in raid and fed into the persistent squad flow.
  • Follower plate overlays help identify friendlies during raids.
  • Current follower hotkeys:
    • F6 - Follow
    • F7 - Hold
    • F8 - Combat
    • F10 - Recover / assist

[tab='Squad Manager']

Squad Manager appears in Messenger as a chat bot and remains the main roster administration surface for the SPT 4.x branch.

Supported chat commands

  • [tt]/help[/tt] - Show command help.
  • [tt]/list[/tt] - List roster members and their current state.
  • [tt]/add [nickname][/tt] - Create a new follower record.
  • [tt]/rename <nickname> <newnickname>[/tt] - Rename a follower.
  • [tt]/delete <nickname>[/tt] - Remove a follower from the roster.
  • [tt]/autojoin <nickname> on|off[/tt] - Toggle whether that follower joins raids automatically.
  • [tt]/kit <nickname> persisted|generated[/tt] - Switch between stored follower gear and generated kits.
  • [tt]/equiplist[/tt] - List your saved player equipment build names.
  • [tt]/equip <nickname> <buildname>[/tt] - Apply one of your saved builds to a follower.
  • [tt]/info <nickname>[/tt] - Show side, autojoin state, kit mode, level, experience, and snapshot status.

Why Messenger?

The point is to keep roster management in the game. You do not need to alt-tab into config files just to check who is active, who is using persisted gear, or who is set to autojoin.

[tab='Inventory Workflow']

Follower inventory is handled through in-game profile flow.

  1. Open Messenger.
  2. Open a managed follower profile from the social/friends list.
  3. Select OPEN INVENTORY.
  4. Inspect the follower's equipped gear and carry containers.
  5. Move items between player and follower inventory.
  6. Equip compatible gear directly into valid follower slots.

The follower side is designed around visible carry space, not an invisible extra stash. The current direction is practical out-of-raid gear management with reliable persistence, clearer errors, and fewer cases where the profile view falls out of sync after a gear change.

[tab='Installation']

Target version: SPT 4.x

Package layout

  • Client: copy the provided BepInEx contents into your SPT install.
  • Server: copy the provided user\mods contents into your SPT install.

Dependencies and recommendations

  • BigBrain is required by the client plugin.
  • Waypoints is strongly recommended for better follower navigation.
  • Large AI overhauls can affect follower behavior and should be treated as compatibility variables.

Basic install reminder

  • Install the client plugin into your SPT BepInEx path.
  • Install the server mod into your SPT user\mods path.
  • Make sure required dependencies are installed before testing followers in raid.

[tab='Compatibility Notes']

Friendly PMCs Reborn touches spawning, squad ownership, bot hostility, movement, social UI, and inventory state. That means compatibility with major AI stacks is an active engineering target, not something that can be promised universally.

Known compatibility priorities

  • BigBrain is part of the active follower command architecture.
  • Waypoints is recommended for navigation/pathing quality.
  • SAIN is a high-impact compatibility surface because it owns combat decisions and enemy targeting.
  • LootingBots can compete for follower behavior, so follower-local compatibility handling is being worked on instead of expensive global patches.

Practical advice

  • Test with a simple mod stack first if follower behavior seems strange.
  • Add large AI mods back one at a time when debugging.
  • If a follower stops obeying commands, note whether they are fighting, looting, healing, or stuck in a behavior layer.
